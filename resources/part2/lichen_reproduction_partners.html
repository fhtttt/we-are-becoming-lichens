Lichen reproduction showing whether fungus and algae travel together (soredia, isidia) or separately (ascospores).

Algae
Fungal hyphae
Soredium (both together)
Both partners disperse together. Each soredium is a ready-made bundle — algal cells wrapped in fungal threads. It can land anywhere and grow into a new lichen without needing to find a partner.
Algae
Fungal hyphae
Isidium (both together)
Both partners disperse together. An isidium is a coral-like outgrowth containing a complete cross-section of the lichen. When it breaks off, it carries algae and fungus in the exact same proportion as the parent thallus.
Ascospore (fungus only)
Free-living alga
Spore + alga = new lichen
Only the fungus disperses. Ascospores carry no algae. A germinating spore must find and capture a compatible free-living alga in the environment — a risky process with no guarantee of success.
